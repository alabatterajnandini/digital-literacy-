\## Project Overview

The Digital Literacy Project is a structured academic initiative designed to develop and demonstrate core competencies required for effective participation in the digital world. Through five carefully designed tasks, the project covers essential areas including visual communication, professional online presence, programming fundamentals, formal digital communication, and cybersecurity awareness.  



This project highlights the practical application of digital tools while fostering critical thinking, ethical responsibility, and lifelong learning. Each task was completed with a focus on quality, creativity, and professionalism, ensuring alignment with industry standards and academic expectations. The overall objective was to build a robust skill set that supports academic success and future professional growth in a technology-driven environment.

\## Detailed Explanation of All 5 Tasks



\### Task 1: Infographic using Canva

In this task, a visually compelling infographic was designed using Canva, a leading graphic design platform. The infographic presents the fundamental components of digital literacy, including information evaluation, ethical online conduct, and effective digital communication.  



The development process involved thorough research, selection of an appropriate layout template, integration of relevant icons and data visualizations, and careful attention to color scheme, typography, and readability. The final output effectively communicates complex ideas in an accessible format. Both the editable Canva link and high-resolution exported files are available in the `task-1/` folder.



\### Task 2: Digital Portfolio (GitHub, LinkedIn, Kaggle)

This task focused on establishing a professional digital footprint across three major platforms.  



\- \*\*GitHub\*\*: A dedicated account was created and optimized to host academic projects, code repositories, and collaborative contributions, showcasing version control proficiency and technical work.  

\- \*\*LinkedIn\*\*: A complete professional profile was developed, featuring an engaging headline, detailed experience summary, academic achievements, and networking connections.  

\- \*\*Kaggle\*\*: A profile was established to participate in data-related challenges, demonstrating analytical thinking and interest in data science.  



Screenshots of the profiles, direct profile links, and a summary of optimization strategies are documented in the `task-2/` folder. This exercise reinforced the importance of maintaining an authentic and professional online presence.



\### Task 3: Coding (HackerRank) + Google Form

Task 3 combined programming practice with digital data collection. Multiple coding challenges were completed on HackerRank to strengthen problem-solving abilities and coding proficiency in Python and related languages. Certificates and solution screenshots for selected problems are included.  



In parallel, a Google Form was designed to collect structured responses regarding digital literacy awareness and tool usage. The form features clear questions, logical flow, and response validation. The form link, sample responses, and analysis summary are provided in the `task-3/` folder. This task demonstrated both technical coding skills and the practical use of digital survey tools.

\### Task 4: Email writing + Social media checklist

Professional email writing skills were practiced by composing a formal email sample adhering to standard business etiquette, including appropriate subject line, salutation, body structure, and closing. The email was prepared for a realistic scenario such as project submission or professional inquiry.  



Additionally, a detailed social media checklist was created to promote responsible online behavior. The checklist addresses privacy management, content appropriateness, security settings, and strategies to mitigate risks. Both the email document and the checklist are located in the `task-4/` folder. These deliverables emphasize clear communication and mindful digital engagement.



\### Task 5: Cybercrime case study + prevention checklist

A thorough case study was conducted on a prevalent cybercrime (phishing attacks), examining its methodology, real-world impact, and consequences for individuals and organizations. The analysis includes root causes, victim effects, and key lessons learned.  



Based on the study, a practical prevention checklist was developed, outlining actionable steps such as password hygiene, two-factor authentication, recognition of suspicious links, and regular software updates. The complete case study report and prevention checklist are available in the `task-5/` folder. This task enhanced understanding of digital threats and proactive defense strategies.
